About Me

Salutations and welcome to my portfolio! I'm Khai Nguyen and I'm a game developer. I'm currently a 4th year Computer Game Science major at UC Irvine and pursuing a career in game design. My passion for video game development stems from two things:

  • a desire to create games that will have a profound impact on lives and
  • to transcend video games as an art form

One of my interests is in Human-Computer Interaction. I hope to learn from HCI methodologies in order to better inform the design of my video games. I'm very curious in what makes people "tick": to find out what drives them, what they hate, what they love. Mastery of such understanding is something that I strive for as a game developer and designer.

I often find myself more of an Informatics person rather than one of Computer Science. For that, I've taken an interest in web development and this website is my first step into that world. Complementary to web development, I'm also interested in UI/UX research. Again, it is my desire to understand others better in order to build something compelling and appealing to individuals. Nothing would satisfy me more than others enjoying something I helped create.

Besides web development technologies, I plan on learning more of Unreal Engine 4. I'm fairly comfortable with working in Unity but I'd like to experience a more complex engine (with C++). Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and After Effects are fairly essential to UI/UX and game development so those are also on my list of things to learn.

Background

I was born in Hanoi, Vietnam but raised in Rosemead and San Gabriel, CA for most of my life. When I was a child, I was happy to have three things: TV, LEGO, and video games. With TV, I loved escaping reality and immersing myself in the cartoon worlds of Cartoon Network and Nickelodeon (among others); The Busy World of Richard Scarry was a favorite of mine. With LEGO, I was constantly wondering about the next thing I wanted to build and the imginary world I wanted to play in; I especially loved building robots and futuristic weapons. With video games, it was both the immersion and challenge that they offered that I loved; Super Mario World and A Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past were quite a challenge to me, actually (I was never able to beat them then). But their worlds kept me engaged and playing (and trying). These forms of entertainment had a profound effect on me - I knew I wanted to create/build something when I grew up.

In high school, I found myself interested in computer science. I tried a programming class (AP Computer Science A in Java) and passed the AP test. As happy as I was with passing, I felt programming was too difficult and switched to engineering instead. I went to Pasadena City College to study mechanical engineering (later switching to electrical) and transfer to a 4-year college. To finish my transfer requirements, I realized I had to finish a C++ class (that meant programming)! Coming from Java, C++ wasn't so bad although pointers were tricky. I managed to pass the class anyway and had a somewhat renewed interest and appreciation for programming.

UC Irvine was one of the four schools I had applied to for electrical engineering. For my backup major, I felt strongly enough about going back to computer science that I chose "computer game science" as my major. Upon entering, I discovered the Video Game Development Club; I have been developing games ever since.
